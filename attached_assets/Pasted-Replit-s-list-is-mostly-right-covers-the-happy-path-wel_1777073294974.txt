Replit’s list is mostly right — covers the happy path well — but it has one minor redundancy and three meaningful gaps you should close now rather than discover later when something breaks silently in production.
The redundancy first: payment_intent.succeeded is duplicative for your architecture. For a subscription business going through Embedded Checkout, the same successful payment fires three events: checkout.session.completed (initial), invoice.paid (the invoice it satisfied), and payment_intent.succeeded (the underlying PI). Your provisioning logic should key off checkout.session.completed for first-time grants and invoice.paid for renewals. payment_intent.succeeded only adds value if you later sell something via raw PaymentIntent (no Checkout, no invoice) — e.g., an a-la-carte one-time charge that isn’t a subscription. Harmless to keep, just expect every renewal to trigger your handler twice. I’d remove it until you have a non-Checkout one-time purchase flow.
Three you should add now:
invoice.payment_action_required — when a customer’s card requires SCA/3DS authentication for a renewal (not the initial purchase, which Checkout handles inline), Stripe pauses the invoice and waits for the customer to complete authentication via the hosted invoice URL. Without this webhook, you have no signal that you need to email the customer “please complete authentication to keep your LEGEND access.” You’ll see invoice.payment_failed after the retries exhaust, but by then the customer has churned silently. This is the #1 silent-churn cause for SCA-region customers (EU/UK).
radar.early_fraud_warning.created — issued by card networks (mostly Visa) when fraud is detected on a charge but before a chargeback is filed. You typically get 24–72 hours of warning. The standard playbook is to proactively refund within that window: you eat the transaction value but avoid the $15 dispute fee and the dispute-rate hit (which, if it climbs above ~0.7%, gets you put on the chargeback monitoring program). For digital goods especially, this is essentially free money to listen for.
charge.dispute.funds_withdrawn — the dispute lifecycle is: created → funds_withdrawn (bank pulls money from your Stripe balance) → closed (won/lost). Replit included created and closed but skipped the funds-withdrawn signal. You want this for accurate balance accounting and to know when you’ve actually been debited, separate from when the dispute was filed.
Conditional add — only if relevant:
customer.subscription.trial_will_end — fires 3 days before a trial converts to paid. Only useful if you offer LEGEND trials. If your launch model is “pay or no LEGEND,” skip this. If you add a trial later, add it then.
payment_method.automatically_updated — when a customer’s card is reissued (new number, same account) and Visa/Mastercard’s account updater service pushes the new card details to Stripe automatically. Without listening, you might see what looks like a churn signal in your own analytics that’s actually just a card refresh. Cheap to listen for, and you may want to fire a “your card on file was updated” email for trust.
Defer until you actually need them:
invoice.upcoming (renewal reminder emails — nice but not critical for a sub at the price point you’re likely targeting), checkout.session.expired (abandoned-cart recovery — meaningful only if you decide to do recovery emails), checkout.session.async_payment_succeeded/_failed (only relevant if you enable SEPA, BACS, or other delayed-settlement payment methods — skip for US-focused card-only launch).
One architectural note that matters more than the event list: every webhook handler must be idempotent against the event ID, and your stripe_processed_events table should be the gate. Stripe will deliver the same event multiple times under retry, and during incident recovery you’ll sometimes manually replay events from the Dashboard. Treating each event as “if I’ve seen this event ID before, ack and return 200” is non-negotiable — it doesn’t matter how thoughtfully you pick events if a duplicate invoice.paid double-extends someone’s LEGEND access.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
